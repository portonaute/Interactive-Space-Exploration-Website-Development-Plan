Space Exploration History

From ancient stargazing to modern space missions, discover humanity's journey to the stars.

Interactive Timeline

Explore key events in space exploration history by selecting different eras.

~3000 BCE

Stonehenge Construction Begins

Early astronomical observatory used to mark solstices and equinoxes.

~1600 BCE

Babylonian Star Catalogs

First systematic observations of the night sky recorded on clay tablets.

~350 BCE

Aristotle's Geocentric Model

Proposed that Earth was the center of the universe with celestial bodies orbiting around it.

~150 CE

Ptolemy's Almagest

Comprehensive astronomical treatise that became the definitive work on astronomy for over a millennium.

~800 CE

Islamic Golden Age Astronomy

Arab astronomers make significant advances in observational astronomy and mathematical techniques.

1543

Copernicus' Heliocentric Model

Published "On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres," proposing that the Sun, not Earth, was at the center of the universe.

1609

Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion

Described how planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus.

1610

Galileo's Telescope Observations

Discovered Jupiter's four largest moons and observed the phases of Venus, supporting the heliocentric model.

1668

Newton's Reflecting Telescope

Invented the first practical reflecting telescope, revolutionizing astronomical observation.

1687

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

Published in "Principia," explaining the motion of planets and providing a unified theory of gravity.

1903

Wright Brothers' First Flight

Achieved the first powered, controlled, and sustained flight of a heavier-than-air aircraft.

1926

Goddard's Liquid-Fueled Rocket

Launched the first liquid-fueled rocket, demonstrating the technology that would enable space travel.

1942

V-2 Rocket First Launch

German scientists developed the first ballistic missile, which became the first human-made object to reach space.

1947

Sound Barrier Broken

Chuck Yeager became the first person to exceed the speed of sound in level flight in the Bell X-1.

1952

International Council of Scientific Unions

Proposed the International Geophysical Year (1957-1958), which would lead to the first satellites.

1957

Sputnik 1 Launch

Soviet Union launched the first artificial satellite, beginning the Space Race.

1961

Yuri Gagarin's Spaceflight

First human to journey into outer space, completing one orbit around Earth.

1962

John Glenn Orbits Earth

First American to orbit Earth, completing three orbits in Friendship 7.

1969

Apollo 11 Moon Landing

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon.
